What's encoded

  • Every bar = one input (email, call, doc, meeting). More inputs = denser sectors. Each bar is a real event.
  • Bar height = score at that moment. Score drops create visible notches. Climbs create peaks. The silhouette IS the score history.
  • Bar width = time gap. Long silence before an input? Wide bar. Rapid-fire inputs? Thin compressed bars. You see the rhythm.
  • Sector size = knowledge depth. Dimensions with more references get physically wider angular space. You instantly see what we know most about.
  • Texture = doc type. Diagonal hatch = email. Horizontal lines = call transcript. Solid = document/PDF. Stipple = meeting. Color stays per-dimension.
  • Intricacy grows outward. Early bars (inner ring) are simple. Later bars subdivide — inner notches show P2V2C2 sub-references. Dense deals look ornate.
  • Stakeholder nodes scale by role. DM is massive. PC is small. ES is medium. The importance hierarchy is visible at a glance.
  • Connection lines link to specific bars — the actual inputs that stakeholder was involved in. Not just the sector, the exact bar.
  • DAP boundary ring tracks milestone progress. Bars extending beyond = ahead of plan.
  • Every deal looks different. Sparse deals are airy. Dense deals are intricate. Uneven deals have lopsided silhouettes. No two are alike.
