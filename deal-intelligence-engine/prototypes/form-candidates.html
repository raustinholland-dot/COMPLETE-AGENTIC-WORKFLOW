Flower Ideal: Full symmetric bloom, all 6 petals equal length, deep green. Deviation: Short or red petals show lagging dimensions. A wilted petal is immediately visceral — you feel something dying.
Gem Ideal: Flawless hexagonal brilliant-cut stone, symmetric facets, bright sparkle. Deviation: Collapsed facets make the gem look chipped or dull. Low-score facets darken toward near-black — like a flawed stone.
Tree Ring Ideal: Even concentric rings of warm heartwood, thick and uniform. Deviation: Thin dark rings show stress periods. Color variation across sectors reveals dimensional imbalance — like drought scarring in real wood.
Mandala Ideal: Perfect 6-fold radial symmetry, all arms equal, deep green. Deviation: Stunted arms break the symmetry. The mandala's geometric precision makes even small imbalances feel wrong — your eye catches asymmetry instantly.
