What to look for

  • Bars grow like vines: Each radial bar is a scoring period. Taller = higher score. Thicker = more documents/activity feeding that dimension. They accumulate outward like growth rings.
  • Score drops are visible: When Pain drops from 3 to 2, the bar literally gets shorter in that ring. You see the dip in the silhouette — a notch, a recession. Climbs create peaks.
  • Color intensity = confidence: Low activity dims produce muted, translucent bars. High activity (lots of emails, calls, docs) produces vivid, saturated bars. You can feel which dimensions have real evidence behind them.
  • Stakeholder arcs: Curved lines connect stakeholder nodes (outer ring) to the dimensions they influence. More connections = more arcs crossing the space. Champion-to-Pain is a thick bright arc. PC-to-Power might be thin and dashed.
  • Time-at-level bands: If a dimension sits at score 3 for 4 weeks, you see 4 consecutive bars at the same height — a plateau band. Rapid climbs create staircase patterns.
  • DAP boundary: The dashed ring shows DAP progress. If the deal is moving faster than the plan, bars extend beyond it. If stalling, the boundary sits ahead of the growth.
  • Week 2 is sparse and delicate. Week 14 is dense, intricate, and alive. The difference should be visceral.
