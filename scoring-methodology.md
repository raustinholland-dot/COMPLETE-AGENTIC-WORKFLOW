# Scoring Methodology — Clearwater Performance Selling (CPS)

This document defines the sales methodology used to evaluate deals. It is a static reference — it only changes if Clearwater changes its sales process.

---

## P2V2C2 RUBRIC

Six dimensions. Each scored 0-5. Total /30. Higher = higher probability of winning.

### Pain (0-5)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | No knowledge of pain |
| 1 | Potential Champion (PC) has shared their situation |
| 2 | PC has admitted a pain |
| 3 | PC has indicated the pain of the Potential Executive Sponsor (PES) |
| 4 | PES has admitted pain |
| 5 | Executive Sponsor (ES) has agreed the pain is great enough to change |

Pain IMPACTS the business and is FELT by the Champion and Executive Sponsor. Describe the pain from either or both perspectives.

**Evidence floor:** Score 3+ if the client uses vivid or emotional language about their situation (e.g., "firefighting," "can't sleep at night," "fox in the hen house"). Score 1 if pain is only implied or inferred — the client must articulate it.

### Power (0-5)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | No idea who is power |
| 1 | Have an indication of a PES |
| 2 | Champion has agreed to take us to PES |
| 3 | Have met with the PES |
| 4 | PES/ES has agreed to DAP |
| 5 | Steps of the DAP are on schedule |

Describe whether we are stuck at the Champion level. Does the ES need board approval? Is there a DM above the ES?

**Evidence floor:** Identify every person by name and role. If we have only met with the Champion and no one else, Power cannot exceed 2 regardless of how positive the Champion is.

### Vision (0-5)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | No idea of needs |
| 1 | We have begun to define needs |
| 2 | Have mapped our capabilities to their needs |
| 3 | They have agreed to proof criteria |
| 4 | ES has agreed to vision of improved future state |
| 5 | ES is painting the vision to others in the organization |

### Value (0-5)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | Benefits of our solution are unknown |
| 1 | Believe their situation is causing pain |
| 2 | Have defined the cost of their pain |
| 3 | Believe our solution can address their pain |
| 4 | ES has shared benefits of our solution with DM |
| 5 | Decision Maker (DM) has agreed to financial terms |

### Change (0-5)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | No one committed to change |
| 1 | PC is open to change |
| 2 | PC confirmed PES will meet with us |
| 3 | PES has agreed they need to change |
| 4 | ES has chosen Clearwater to lead change |
| 5 | ES convinced DM they must change |

**Evidence floor:** Score 3+ if the client has requested a proposal, updated scope/counts, or asked for next steps. These are change signals.

### Control (0-5)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | Buying process unknown |
| 1 | Champion (C) has described the process as C knows it |
| 2 | Have met with a PES |
| 3 | Pain is admitted and vision created for ES |
| 4 | ES has agreed to DAP |
| 5 | DAP is complete |

## KEY CLIENT ROLES

| Abbrev | Role | Definition |
|--------|------|------------|
| PC | Potential Champion | May advocate for us but hasn't committed |
| C | Champion | Wants you to win. Provides access to power. Sells on your behalf when you're not around. |
| PES | Potential Executive Sponsor | Everything a Champion does PLUS can find money/budget and provide access to additional power. Has not yet chosen us. |
| ES | Executive Sponsor | PES who has chosen us. Can support only one company. Will buy into a DAP. |
| DM | Ultimate Decision Maker | Has final say. Likely defers to ES. |
| EB | Economic Buyer | Approves the price and financial terms. |

Every person on the deal must be assigned a role. If the role is unclear, note it and state what evidence would clarify.

## SALES STAGES & CRITICAL ACTIVITY STAGE (CAS)

CAS represents the NEXT required verifiable outcome. The current verified deal state is the CAS immediately prior to the one listed. A deal does not advance until the CAS outcome is verifiably completed.

**Stage is a LAGGING indicator.** If CAS outcomes don't support the stated Stage, challenge it.

**Nuanced CAS:** Recognize 3A completion via collaborative agreement; pivot immediately to 3B.

| Stage | What Must Be True | What Is NOT True Yet |
|-------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Discovery** | Pain articulated. Potential Champion identified. | No solution proposed. No validation criteria. |
| **Qualify** | Problem confirmed by Champion. Willingness to change validated. Buying intent identified. | No approach co-created. |
| **Prove** | Solution approach co-created. Proof/validation criteria defined or being validated. | No commercial terms. |
| **Negotiate** | Solution approach accepted. Legal/procurement/contracting active. | Contract not executed. |
| **Close** | All approvals obtained. Contract executed. | — |

### Prove Stage — Non-Negotiable Sequencing

1. Build Approach Document (internal — NO pricing shared)
2. Schedule Validation / Approach Call
3. Conduct Validation / Approach Call
4. Send Approach Document WITH pricing

**Pricing shared before Validation Call = sequencing violation. Flag it.**

**Document discipline:** Do not recommend emailing pricing prior to it being presented on a live call via the Approach Document.

## DUAL ACTION PLAN (DAP)

The DAP is the single most important control mechanism in the deal.

### What a DAP Is
A client-agreed sequence of milestones required to reach contract execution or project start. It is the joint project plan between Clearwater and the client.

### DAP Quality Requirements

**Valid DAP must include:**
- Client AND Clearwater ownership per step
- Dates tied to external drivers (not internal wishes)
- Alignment to the client's buying process
- Linkage to a decision or approval step

**Invalid DAP signals:**
- Internal-only milestones with no client confirmation
- Dates not confirmed by the client
- No linkage to a decision or approval step
- Generic dates with no rationale

### DAP Status Categories
- **Confirmed by client** — client has agreed to milestones and timeline
- **Internal draft** — Clearwater has projected milestones, client has not confirmed
- **Not yet discussed** — no DAP exists

### Full 14-Milestone DAP Template

Every DAP must track against these milestones. Not all apply to every deal — mark N/A where appropriate. **Never truncate the list.**

```
[Date] - Initial Discovery [STATUS]
[Date] - Define Current State and Desired Future State [STATUS]
[Date] - Co-Create Session [STATUS]
[Date] - Present Draft Approach [STATUS]
[Date] - Present Updated Approach Based on Feedback [STATUS]
[Date] - Go / No Go Decision [STATUS]
[Date] - Submit for Budget Approval [STATUS]
[Date] - Send MSA/SOW [STATUS]
[Date] - Client Initial Review of MSA/SOW [STATUS]
[Date] - Redlines Sent to Clearwater [STATUS]
[Date] - Call to Review Redlines [STATUS]
[Date] - Finalize Redlines and Launch DocuSign [STATUS]
[Date] - Execute Contract [STATUS]
[Date] - Initial Engagement Meeting / Project Start [STATUS]
```

STATUS values: COMPLETED / PENDING / NOT STARTED / N-A / PROJECTED

**Timeline rules:**
- Anchor from the initial discovery date and progress forward
- Multiple milestones covered in one interaction share the same date
- Stretch timelines realistically for complex deals (2 weeks for legal review, etc.)
- Execute Contract must follow DocuSign by 1-2 days
- If dates are not client-confirmed, project logical future dates and label PROJECTED

### Next Step Mandate
The Next Step must ALWAYS be a client-focused action, never an internal task.

## FORECAST CATEGORY

Only meaningful for deals closing THIS quarter.

| Category | Criteria |
|----------|----------|
| Pipeline | Deal in motion, no verifiable path to close this quarter |
| Upside | Possible this quarter but not controlled |
| Commit | Buyer control established, verifiable path to close |
| Closed | Contract executed |

- Commit requires buyer control and a verifiable path to close
- Optimism never upgrades forecast
- Must downgrade when control, scope, or timing degrades
- Forecast Category does NOT equal Deal Health

---

## DEAL CONTROL SIGNALS

**Positive:** client-agreed DAP, access to ES, clear buying/approval process, client requesting next steps, client sharing internal constraints or timelines

**Negative:** missed milestones without reset, Champion-only communication, budget/approval ambiguity, going dark, competitor presence without differentiation, legal stall with no resolution path

---

## CURRENT STATE / FUTURE STATE

When documenting, use three categories:
- **ORGANIZATION** — team structure, staffing, internal capacity, governance
- **ACTIVITIES** — what they do today for security/compliance/risk (or don't do)
- **TECHNOLOGY** — environment, tools, platforms, infrastructure

Prefer facts over narrative. Mark UNKNOWN for gaps — never fabricate.

---

## STAKEHOLDER SUCCESS CRITERIA

Capture what each stakeholder has stated they need from the engagement:
- **TO THE BOARD:** what executive leadership needs to see (ROI justification, risk posture baseline, etc.)
- **[Name, Role]:** their stated success criteria in their own framing

If success criteria have not been explicitly stated by the client, label UNCONFIRMED.
